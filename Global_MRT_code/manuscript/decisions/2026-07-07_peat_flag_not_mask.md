# Peatland: flag, don't mask

**Date:** 2026-07-07 **Status:** Adopted

## Question (reviewer concern)

Organic (peat) soils may distort the turnover-time analysis: the steady-state,
mineral-soil framing behind tau = SOC / carbon-input does not really apply to
Histosols, where accumulation is not at steady state and the carbon balance is
dominated by waterlogging and slow anaerobic decomposition. A reviewer asked us
to filter peat observations (the maps themselves could stay).

## Decision

**Keep Histosol observations in the statistical analysis; do not interpret tau
over Histosol-dominated areas on the maps.** Concretely:

- The model fit and all variance-decomposition results (main text) are run on the
  full observation set, Histosols included. They are honest data and excluding
  them changes nothing material (see numbers below).
- On the predicted-tau maps, Histosol-dominated areas are flagged (hatch/outline)
  with a caption stating that tau is not interpreted there because the
  steady-state mineral-soil framing does not apply to organic soils.
- We do **not** hole-punch or globally peat-filter the pipeline.

We say "Histosol-classified sites," not "all peat": the 0.1-degree modal soil class
misses thin or degraded peat, so this is a conservative, well-defined subset, not a
claim to have removed every peat pixel.

## Why (reasoning)

The objection is fundamentally about *interpretation of tau over organic soils*,
not about *contamination of the statistics*. The relative/spatial claim of the
paper (the beyond-climate zonality) is unaffected by whether ~1% of rows are
organic soils, and we can show that directly. A consistent global peat-drop would
require re-running the entire downstream chain (model fit 13; commonality / Moran /
scale / provenance / block-size 13c-13i; extrapolation and maps 14, 15, 15b, 16;
ALE 18; CMIP comparison 20-20c), shifting every number and figure by a hair and
forcing a full co-author re-review, to chase an effect the sensitivity test shows
is negligible. Flag-not-mask both satisfies the reviewer (filter is unnecessary
because robustness is demonstrated; maps stay, now flagged) and is the more correct
treatment.

## Supporting numbers (sensitivity test)

Dropping `soilclass_wrb_name == "Histosols"` and re-running the full commonality /
Shapley / Moran decomposition (`MRT_13C_PEAT=drop`), versus the main run:

| Run | n (common rows) | full R^2 | climate R^2 | beyond-climate gap | Moran's I | Shapley C / E / B / L (%) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Main (peat kept)** | 154,675 | 0.380 | 0.307 | **7.31 pp** | **0.252** | 35.1 / 32.8 / 22.3 / 9.7 |
| **Histosols dropped** | 153,518 | 0.381 | 0.314 | **6.68 pp** | **0.248** | 35.7 / 32.7 / 22.5 / 9.1 |

Dropping Histosols removes 1,157 rows from the common analysis set (~2,573
Histosol-classified rows, ~1.3%, exist in the full `12b_model_ready.rds`; most are
already outside the biology-limited common-row footprint). The beyond-climate gap
moves 7.31 -> 6.68 pp and Moran's I 0.252 -> 0.248; the Shapley shares are stable to
<1 pp. The signal is not a peat artefact.

## Reproduce

```
MRT_13C_PEAT=drop Rscript Global_MRT_code/13c_commonality_validation.R
# -> outputs/13c_decomposition_nopeat.rds  (Shapley, full/clim R^2, config)
Rscript Global_MRT_code/13j_input_sensitivity.R
# -> the nopeat row of outputs/13j_input_sensitivity.csv (adds Moran's I)
```

## In the manuscript

- Cite the SI table `tab:peat_sensitivity` (generated by
  `13k_input_sensitivity_appendix.R` -> `outputs/13k_peat_sensitivity_table.tex`).
- One-sentence robustness statement (Limitations or Methods): "Excluding the ~1.3%
  Histosol-classified sites leaves the beyond-climate gap at 6.7 pp and Moran's I at
  0.248 (Table~\\ref{tab:peat_sensitivity}), so the result is not driven by organic
  soils; if anything the gap decreases slightly, i.e. organic soils modestly add to
  rather than inflate the beyond-climate signal."
- Map caption flag as above. The peat sensitivity is **not** a shown figure panel
  in the input-sensitivity appendix figure (that figure is reserved for the three
  input *definitions*; peat is a different, data-filter axis).

## Revisit if

We would switch to a consistent global peat-drop only as a *framing* choice — if we
decide the paper should scope itself explicitly to mineral soils rather than carry
peat as a flagged caveat. The numbers do not require it; this is a narrative call.
See [[decision-input-term-management]] (input-side circularity rules) for the
related coefficient-vs-data-gap reasoning.
